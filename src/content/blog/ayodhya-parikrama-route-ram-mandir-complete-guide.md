# Ayodhya Parikrama Route - Ram Mandir Complete Guide

Parikrama (circumambulation) of Ram Mandir is a revered practice. Here's a practical guide and how your stay at Sri Janaki Mahal Trust supports it.

## Parikrama Overview

- **Route**: Circumambulation around the Ram Mandir complex
- **Distance**: Varies—typically a few kilometers
- **Timing**: Early morning or evening are popular; cooler and less crowded

**Current route and rules** may change—check with temple authorities or local guides when you visit.

## Tips for Parikrama

- **Footwear**: Comfortable shoes
- **Hydration**: Carry water
- **Pace**: Go at a comfortable speed
- **Respect**: Follow temple guidelines and [cultural etiquette](/blog/ayodhya-cultural-etiquette-dress-code-guide)

## From Janaki Mahal Trust

The trust is in Karsewakpuram, near Ram Mandir. You can walk to the parikrama start point. After parikrama, return to the trust for rest and meals. See [location](/location) and [post-darshan rest](/blog/janaki-mahal-trust-post-darshan-rest-recovery-tips).
