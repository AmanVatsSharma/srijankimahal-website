# Ayodhya Railway Station Waiting Room & Facilities (Pilgrim Guide)

Ayodhya Junction (AY) is the main rail hub for pilgrims. Here's what to expect and how to plan your arrival before check-in at Sri Janaki Mahal Trust.

## Waiting Rooms

- **General waiting halls**: Available for passengers; may be crowded during peak hours
- **AC waiting lounge**: Sometimes available; check at enquiry
- **Ladies waiting room**: Separate facility for women travelers

## Retire Rooms

Indian Railways offers retire rooms at many stations. At Ayodhya Junction, check availability and booking through IRCTC or station enquiry. Useful if you arrive very late or have an early morning connection.

## Food and Refreshments

- Station canteen and stalls
- Vendors on platforms
- For better meals, proceed to your accommodation—Sri Janaki Mahal Trust offers [meals included](/blog/sri-janaki-mahal-trust-meals-food-guide) with your stay.

## Planning Your Arrival

- **Check-in time**: Sri Janaki Mahal Trust typically allows check-in from 12 noon. See [check-in and check-out timings](/blog/sri-janaki-mahal-trust-check-in-check-out-timings).
- **Early arrival**: You may wait at the station or at the trust’s common area if rooms are not yet ready.
- **Late night arrival**: Confirm with [official contact](/contact-number) that someone can receive you.

## From Station to Janaki Mahal Trust

Auto or e-rickshaw takes about 15–20 minutes. Directions: [location from Ayodhya Junction](/location/from-ayodhya-junction).
